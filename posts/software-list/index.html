CGI Free Software List

A list of free software with amazing capabilities with regards to creating computer generated imagery.

Published on October 12, 2022
451 words 3 min read

Software
Image by Andrew Ridley

Introduction #

Software used for creating digital images is not what it used to be. In the last decade alone our tools have evolved dramatically, giving powers that was once reserved for entire teams of artists to a single person working from their home computer. However along with that shift we also got the abhorring Software-as-a-Service (or SaaS) subscription model which alongside its seemingly affordable pricing brings spyware for software vendors to be able to check our computers for licenses. Well if you are like me, rejoice, because like we previously talked about software used for CGI is not what it used to be.

Nowadays we have a plethora of options to choose from, many of which come close to their proprietary counterpart’s capabilities in most ways even if they lack the machine learning powered features that some vendors have enriched their software with. The best example of this is Blender, which has been used to create more than 15 high quality movies with studios such as Ubisoft and Barnstorm VFX using it in their production pipelines for shows you may have seen such as The Man in the High Castle.

Free and Open Source with an asterisk #

The following list contains programs that are free as in free to access, and modify to your heart’s content. They are released under a variety of licenses some of which restrict the ways in which the software can be used or redistributed but they are united under the flag of open source.

# Name Description Link
1 Blender General purpose 3D
2 Krita Digital painting and image processing
3 Inkscape Vector Graphics, Illustrator alternative
4 Texture Lab Material authoring, Designer alternative
5 Material Maker Material authoring, Designer alternative
6 ArmorPaint Look Development, Painter alternative
7 ArmorLab AI Powered Texture Authoring, Alchemist alternative
8 Gaffer Look Development, Layout and Rendering
9 LuxCoreRender Rendering
10 Moonray Rendering
11 Natron Digital Compositing
12 KDEnlive Video Editing
13 Watchtower Project Management

Notes #

If you are familiar with the field of CGI you may notice that with the gamut of options mentioned above, we today have sufficient resources in the FOSS ecosystem to run a small studio without paying a cent for software. Add to that list Linux, Nextcloud, Gitlab and BigBlueButton and you may scale your capabilities much as you like, on premises or otherwise for very little cost, depending ofcourse on the type of projects you are working for and the services you are offering.
